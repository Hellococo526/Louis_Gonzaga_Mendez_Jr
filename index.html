Background Information

Louis Gonzaga Mendez Jr. was born on July 14, 1915, in Denver, Colorado. He was an exceptionally intelligent individual, graduating at the top of his high school class. In 1941, he joined the U.S. Army and was recognized for his bravery, often described as a "hero" for his courageous acts.

During World War II, he served in the 82nd Airborne Division. On June 5â€“6, 1944, he and his crew participated in the largest airborne attack in history, being dropped behind German lines to destroy enemy supplies and disrupt communications. Their mission was a success, achieving its objectives.

After the war, Louis furthered his education, graduating from the Command and General Staff College and the Industrial College of the Armed Forces. In the 1960s, he served as a regimental commander until his retirement in 1985.

Beyond his military career, Louis was a family man with a wife, six daughters, and six sons. He passed away on September 19, 2001, at his home due to a stroke.

Awards

Mendez was awarded the Distinguished Service Cross, second only to the Medal of Honor. After the fighting in Normandy, Mendez continued to lead his battalion, which took part in Operation Market Garden, in September 1944.
